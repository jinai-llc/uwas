Supplementary Figure S1. Universal-Wide Association Study (UWAS) Analytical Pipeline
Step-by-step workflow of the UWAS framework, an AI-augmented multi-modal analytical pipeline with knowledge regularization for comorbidity-stratified prediction of Alzheimer's disease.
Step 1
ADNI Database Extraction
4,792 visits from the Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative (ADNI) across 58 acquisition sites. Clinical demographics, genetic markers, T1 structural MRI, and diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) extracted.
Step 2
Analytic Cohort Assembly
CN = 749  |  AD = 385  |  58 sites
Longitudinal deduplication: latest visit (AD), baseline (CN). Leakage exclusion: diagnostic labels and cognitive test scores removed from feature pool.
MCI subjects excluded to maximize CN vs AD discriminative signal
Step 3
Multi-Modal Feature Assembly
14,229 candidate variables classified into four modality pools via suffix-based annotation. Feature types automatically detected: continuous standardized, binary retained, categorical encoded (ordinal/one-hot).

Step 4
Longitudinal MRI Feature Transformation
For neuroimaging features derived from longitudinal MRI acquisitions, cross-sectional CN vs AD comparisons may be confounded by normal age-related atrophy. To address this, population-level age-interaction beta coefficients (beta_int) were computed via regression modeling:
Feature ~ beta_0 + beta_1(Age) + beta_2(Diagnosis) + beta_3(Age x Diagnosis)
The interaction term beta_3 quantifies how the age-feature relationship differs between CN and AD groups. A significant beta_3 indicates that the feature's trajectory with age is altered by disease status — capturing disease-accelerated change rather than normal aging. These beta_3 values were used to evaluate the relevance of longitudinal imaging features at the population level, enabling Step 6 to rank MRI features by disease-specific signal independent of age confounding. Features with non-significant beta_3 were deprioritized; features with large beta_3 were retained as candidates with disease-accelerated change.
Example: Hippocampal Volume
Both CN and AD decline with age, but AD declines faster → large negative beta_3 → strong AD association
Example: Ventricular Volume
Both expand with age, but AD expands faster → large positive beta_3 → strong AD association
Step 5
AI-Guided Feature Engineering NOVEL
OpenAI GPT models (GPT-4o-mini utilized to accommodate 14,229 features) evaluate each candidate variable for biological relevance to AD and extracts cross-modal biological relationships, assembling a feature validation network.
LLM Validation
AD-relevance scoring (0–10) per feature based on established literature
Feature Network
Redundancy filtering via pairwise biological relationship graph
Knowledge Imputation
Missing values estimated from biologically connected features, not median fill
Step 6
Modality-Separated Pre-Filtering with Knowledge Regularization
Mann-Whitney U test applied within each modality pool independently to evaluate marginal association with AD. Prevents high-dimensional MRI features from dominating through numerosity. Longitudinal imaging features filtered by beta_3 from Step 4. Clinical features knowledge-regularized (λk = 5.0): statistical association weighted by LLM-derived biological plausibility score from Step 5. Features with low plausibility (e.g., race, scan parameters) downweighted; biologically grounded features (e.g., genetic risk, hippocampal volume) preserved.

Step 7
Backward Feature Elimination
Iterative removal within each modality independently. At each step, the feature whose removal causes the smallest AUC decrement is dropped. Yields the minimal feature set preserving discriminative performance per modality.
Clinical: 65 → 8  |  T1 MRI: 8,914 → 10  |  DTI: 5,238 → 10
Step 8
Leave-One-Site-Out (LOSO) Cross-Validation
10 largest ADNI sites (each ≥30 subjects). For each fold, one site held out as test; L2 logistic regression (C=1.0) trained on remaining sites. LOSO-averaged importance weights computed across all folds.
Deterministic and reproducible — no stochastic variability from random fold assignment
Step 9
HTN × DM Stratified Analysis
Subjects stratified into four subgroups by hypertension (HTN) and diabetes (DM) status. Per-strata backward elimination and mixed-modality combination search conducted independently.
